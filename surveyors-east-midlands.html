Local knowledge

One region, many kinds of home

The East Midlands runs from Victorian terraces to modern estates — and each city has its own quirks our local surveyors know well.

  • Leicester — shrinkable clay soils that bring seasonal cracking and subsidence risk, plus the period villas of Stoneygate.
  • Nottingham — famous sandstone cellars and caves, and former coalfield ground to the north.
  • Derby — flood risk near the River Derwent and the Rolls-Royce/railway terraces around the city.
