Local knowledge

Surveyors who know Nottingham homes

Nottingham's housing is wonderfully varied — and every era has its quirks. Our local RICS surveyors know exactly what to check, street by street.

  • Victorian & Edwardian terraces in Sneinton, Lenton, Forest Fields and Hyson Green — rising damp, roof spread and bowing bay windows.
  • The city's famous sandstone — cellars, caves and rock-cut voids beneath older Nottingham properties that need a careful eye.
  • Former coalfield ground across north Nottinghamshire — we factor in mining-legacy and subsidence considerations.
  • New builds in West Bridgford & the waterside — snagging and developer defects before you complete.

Surveys in Nottingham, answered

How quickly can you survey a home in Nottingham?

We usually attend Nottingham properties within a few days of booking and return your full report within 5–7 days of the inspection.

Do you check Nottingham's caves, cellars and sandstone?

Yes. Many older Nottingham homes sit on or near sandstone with cellars and rock-cut voids. Our surveyors assess accessible cellars and flag anything that needs a specialist, so you buy with eyes open.

Do you cover the wider Nottinghamshire area?

We do — across all NG postcodes, including Gedling, Bingham, Hucknall and out towards the former coalfield, where we factor in mining-legacy and ground considerations.
