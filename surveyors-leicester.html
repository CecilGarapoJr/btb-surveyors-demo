Local knowledge

Surveyors who know Leicester homes

Leicester's housing is wonderfully varied — and every era has its quirks. Our local RICS surveyors know exactly what to check, street by street.

  • Victorian & Edwardian terraces in Clarendon Park, Highfields and Belgrave — rising damp, roof spread and tired bay windows.

  • Shrinkable clay soils across Leicester — seasonal cracking and subsidence risk we assess carefully, especially near mature trees.
  • New-build estates in Hamilton, Ashton Green & Oadby — snagging and developer defects before you complete.

Leicester questions

Surveys in Leicester, answered

How quickly can you survey a home in Leicester?

We usually attend Leicester properties within a few days of booking and return your full report within 5–7 days of the inspection.

Do you check subsidence risk on Leicester's clay soils?

Yes. Much of Leicester sits on shrinkable clay, which can cause seasonal movement. Our surveyors assess cracking and movement, note the proximity of mature trees and seasonal clay shrinkage, and recommend monitoring if there's any sign of concern — so you buy with eyes open.

Do you cover the wider Leicestershire area?

We do — across all LE postcodes, including Oadby, Wigston, Syston and Birstall, and out across the county.
