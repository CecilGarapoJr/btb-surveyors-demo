The right level comes down to the home

The Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) defines three survey levels. The deeper the level, the more thorough the inspection — and the more it tells you about an older, larger or more unusual property. As a rule of thumb: modern home in good order, a Level 2; older, larger or altered home, a Level 3; a brand-new build, a snagging survey; one specific worry, a specific defect survey.

Choosing a survey, answered

What's the difference between Level 1, 2 and 3?

A Level 1 (Condition) is a basic overview for newer, standard homes. A Level 2 (HomeBuyer) adds advice, risks and a closer look — the right choice for most conventional properties. A Level 3 (Building) is the most thorough, explaining the cause and repair of every defect on older, larger or altered homes.

Is a more expensive survey always better?

No — the best survey is the one that matches the property. Paying for a Level 3 on a modern flat in good order is usually unnecessary, while a Level 1 on a Victorian terrace could miss important issues. Match the level to the age and condition of the home.

Do I need a snagging survey as well as a HomeBuyer?

For a brand-new build, a snagging survey is the right tool — it focuses on finish quality and developer defects rather than the kind of condition issues a HomeBuyer covers on older homes.

What if I only have one specific concern?

Then you don't need a full survey at all. A specific defect survey investigates a single issue — such as damp or a crack — diagnoses the cause and sets out the remedy with rough costs.
