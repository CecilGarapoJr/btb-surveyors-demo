01The opportunity

Great firm. Brochure website. Leaking leads.

BTB has everything that wins in this market — RICS regulation, 1,200+ surveys, a 4.8 rating and fair, transparent pricing. But the current site is built to inform, not to convert. The high-intent traffic that should be booking with you is instead being captured by comparison sites (Compare My Move, reallymoving, RICS Find a Surveyor) and sold back to firms like yours as paid leads.

⚡ The market reality78% of survey customers buy from the first firm that responds — and instant-quote calculators lift website conversion by 200–400% (one comparable trade went 1.8% → 7.2% in a month).
❌ Where you leak todayNo instant price (just a slow form), no local area pages, reviews claimed but not shown, one CTA type, no speed-to-lead, thin content moat.
✅ The fixOwn the instant quote, win the 15-minute callback race, and capture local + informational search — so you stop renting demand and keep the full margin.

06From draft to WordPress

"How simple is the WordPress build?"

Short answer: very. These drafts are the design system — clean HTML and one stylesheet, no proprietary page-builder mess. Converting to a WordPress theme is a well-trodden, low-risk path:

1
Approve structure & feel (you're here). Sign off the sitemap and look from these drafts. Cheap to change now, expensive to change later.
2
Pick the WordPress route. Either a lightweight custom block theme built straight from this CSS, or a clean builder (e.g. Bricks/GeneratePress) styled to match — both reuse this exact design.
3
Wire the dynamic bits. The quote calculator connects to a survey CRM (Survey Booker / I Need Leads) for the 15-minute callback; reviews pull live from Google; area & guide pages become editable templates you can add to yourself.
4
Launch & measure. Go live on Phase 1, instrument conversion + speed-to-lead, then iterate.

Because the visual decisions are already locked in code, the WordPress stage is mostly templating and plumbing — not redesign. That keeps it fast and keeps the cost predictable.
