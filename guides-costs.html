What it costs

What drives the price, and what to expect in 2026

Three things mainly set the cost of a survey: the size of the property, the type of survey, and to a lesser degree the location. A larger home takes longer to inspect and write up, so it costs more. A deeper survey level costs more than a lighter one. And prices flex a little by region. The single biggest lever, though, is matching the right survey level to the property — buy the level you need, not the most expensive one available.

As a guide for 2026, a Level 1 Condition Report typically starts around £250, a Level 2 HomeBuyer survey from £420, and a Level 3 Building Survey from £650. Those are realistic starting points for a typical home; a larger or more complex property will sit higher. There are no surprise add-ons with us — the quote you receive is the price you pay.

On timing, a survey rarely holds up a purchase. We usually attend the property within a few days of booking, and your written report follows within roughly 5–7 working days of the inspection — often sooner for a Level 2. If you are working to a tight chain deadline, tell us and we will do our best to prioritise. The full breakdown by level is on our compare survey types page.

Good to know

Cost & timing questions, answered

How much should I budget?

For a typical home in 2026, budget from around £250 for a Level 1 Condition Report, from £420 for a Level 2 HomeBuyer survey, and from £650 for a Level 3 Building Survey. Larger or more complex properties cost more. The right figure depends on the specific property, which is why our instant quote gives you an exact price in under a minute rather than a vague range.

Can I get the survey quicker?

Often, yes. We usually attend within a few days of booking and return the report within 5–7 working days. If you are up against a chain or exchange deadline, let us know when you book — we will do our best to bring the inspection forward and prioritise the write-up. A Level 2 typically turns around faster than a more detailed Level 3.

Is a cheaper survey worse?

Not necessarily — but be careful what you are comparing. A cheaper quote may be for a lighter survey level, a smaller property, or it may not include the same detail or surveyor access. The thing to check is that the survey is carried out by a RICS-qualified surveyor and that the level matches your property. A bargain Level 1 on an old cottage is no saving if it misses the very problems a Level 3 would have caught.
