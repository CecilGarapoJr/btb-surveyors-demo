Avoid HMRC problems

Why an estate-agent figure can cause trouble

It's tempting to use a free agent appraisal, but for inheritance tax that can create problems later. HMRC can challenge an inaccurate figure, and the consequences fall on the executors.

  • An agent may quote a marketing price, not a true market value
  • An under-valuation can trigger an HMRC enquiry and penalties
  • An over-valuation can mean the estate pays too much inheritance tax
  • A Red Book valuation by an RICS Registered Valuer is properly evidenced and defensible
  • If the property later sells for more, an accurate base figure protects the executors

Good to know

Probate valuation questions, answered

What date is the property valued at?

For probate and inheritance tax, the property is valued at the open-market value as at the date of death, regardless of when the inspection takes place or when the property is eventually sold.

Will HMRC accept your valuation?

Yes. A Red Book valuation by an RICS Registered Valuer is exactly the kind of independent, evidenced figure HMRC expects for inheritance tax. It is defensible and stands up to scrutiny if the estate is reviewed.

How quickly can you provide it?

We understand timing matters when administering an estate. We usually attend within a few working days and return the completed report shortly after, so probate isn't held up.

What if there are several properties in the estate?

We can value multiple properties as part of the same instruction, each with its own date-of-death valuation and supporting evidence. Just let us know the addresses when you request a quote.
